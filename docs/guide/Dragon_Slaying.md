# Dragon Slaying Guide

When an enemy kingdom sends a dragon against yours, killing it quickly limits the cumulative tick damage it deals. The goal is a coordinated, distributed send across the kingdom — not a few provinces sacrificing everything.

See [Dragons](../main/Dragons.md) for the full mechanics reference, including dragon HP values and per-dragon effect details.

## How Damage Works

Each troop sent deals damage equal to its **higher** of offensive or defensive value (the two are not combined). A soldier with 3/0 deals 3 damage; an offensive or defensive specialist at 10/0 or 0/10 deals 10 — over three times as much per unit. Race-specific specialists can be even higher (see [Units](../main/Units.md) and [Races](../main/Race.md)).

Troops sent to slay a dragon **die permanently** — they do not return. This is the most important thing to keep in mind when deciding what to send.

- **Specialists (offensive or defensive)** are far more efficient per unit. If you have the gold and enough time, draft soldiers and train them into specs before sending — the damage difference is substantial.
- **Raw soldiers** are fine when you need to send immediately and can't wait out the training time (24 ticks base, modified by race and buildings).
- **Elites** deal even more damage per unit but are expensive to lose permanently. Reserve them for desperate situations or if you're overpopped and would lose them to desertion anyway.

## Who Should Send and How Much

The principle: anyone with a comfortable defense buffer beyond what they need to stay safe in war can peel off a portion for the dragon.

- **Provinces with a healthy defense surplus** can safely commit a portion without becoming an easy target. Keep enough home to avoid being double-tapped.
- **Overpopped (OP) provinces** should prioritise the dragon. Units that would otherwise desert deal real damage instead of disappearing for nothing — this is the clearest win in any dragon fight.
- **War-focused attackers** should contribute something but not at the expense of offensive capability. A partial send still moves the HP down; a full offense send for the sake of a faster kill is almost never worth it.
- **Spread the load.** Higher-defense provinces contributing a meaningful chunk barely affects their war readiness; collective sends add up fast. A few players suiciding full offenses to save 1–2 ticks is rarely the right call.

*Example:* a province comfortably above the defense threshold for their war situation might send 8–10% of their home defense force — enough to matter, not enough to expose themselves.

## Timing and Urgency

Dragons deal burst damage on arrival, then periodic damage every 6 ticks. The longer one lives, the more cumulative harm it causes — early sends matter more than a later surge.

Since sent troops die permanently, don't commit offense you'll need for the next war wave. Defense buffer troops and OP surplus are the right units to send, not the offensive specialists you plan to attack with.

Assess after the first round of sends to see how much HP remains, then decide whether the situation calls for a heavier commitment or if distributed sends over a few hours will do the job.

## Coordination Principles

- **Assess, then escalate.** An initial wave of sends tells you how much HP is left. Reassess before committing heavier resources.
- **Dragons are a priority but not THE priority** — unless the kingdom decides to race the kill, losing war offensive capability to save a tick or two is usually a bad trade. Having no offense is bad.
- **Communicate before suiciding offense.** If someone wants to go all-in, the kingdom should offer to compensate them in gold or soldiers so the send makes strategic sense rather than just burning their army.

## Dragon-Specific Notes

Each dragon's effects shape who should prioritise the kill and what active counter-moves are available.

### Emerald

Two separate threats. The passive debuffs (+25% Military Casualties, -25% Combat Gains) are always active — every battle while it's alive costs the victim kingdom more troops in defense and less land on offense. The tick damage separately destroys 1.5% of the victim's troops at home every 6 ticks, plus reduces Building and Specialist Credits by 40%.

Key timing insight: if the enemy's armies are currently committed on a wave, there's a window to kill the dragon before they can wave again — eliminating the casualty amplifier before it compounds losses on the next hit.

### Ruby

Tick damage only destroys offensive specialists at home. Attackers who regularly keep their offense out on attacks naturally protect those units — active offense is self-shielding against Ruby's periodic drain. Still, the -12.5% Military Effectiveness and +20% Military Wages create sustained pressure on both attack capability and economy.

### Amethyst

Strips all active self-spells on arrival, immediately — any protective buffs you had are gone the moment it lands. It then kills 2% of wizards and thieves every 6 ticks. T/M-heavy kingdoms should treat this as top priority.

### Topaz

Destroys 10% of buildings and 20% of current gold every 6 ticks. The active counter for gold: spend it aggressively (train troops, draft soldiers) before each tick fires rather than letting it pool and get destroyed. Rebuilding during a Topaz dragon is counterproductive — you'd just rebuild what gets torn down next tick.

### Sapphire

Destroys 25% of current runes per tick — geometric decay that melts a large rune stockpile fast. Better to burn runes on spells preemptively than let them sit and be destroyed. The −1 Mana Recovery and −35% WPA/TPA effectiveness compound the disruption for mystic-heavy builds.
